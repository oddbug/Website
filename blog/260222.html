One week on Nekoweb
Febuary 22, 2026

Today marks one week that I created my account and site on Nekoweb. I am switching from Neocities, although slowly. I started making my site on Neocities before I even knew Nekoweb existed, it did start the same year so I didn't learn about it yet. I started making a personal website to express myself and have been on Neocities for almost two years.

It's after about a year and a half that I started wanting to switch to Nekoweb because it allows more things than Neocities, I had the external scripts more in mind. I also wanted to switch for the community. Neocities and Nekoweb are both website hosts, but Nekoweb has more of a community. I started interacting with Nekoweb users and that made me want to switch.

My process is that I just restarted everything from scratch now that I know what I'm doing, definitely much better from when I first started. It's a big cleanup at the same time. A fresh start does feel nice, and I'm already learning more things with how much the Nekoweb community has a bunch of different guides right there on the site and the Discord server, all from still active people you can very much just ask questions to. Everyone is friendly and willing to help. Only one week and I already forget about Neocities honestly. Don't worry to any people from my Neocities site, that one will eventually redirect here once I feel it's ready.

For my chat that I put in the corner of the home page, I tried to use Chattable for it being fully customizable but the messages weren't working. I was able to send one message, then after that, it stopped working. I couldn't send any messages while I was testing things. I tried going on to someone else's chattable chat and I couldn't even send any messages there either. I get it's been on and off because of attacks, the website says that, but it's personally kind of annoying so I decided to just use Cbox again. Maybe I'd use Chattable one day if that attacker gets a life.

I learned how to make an RSS feed since that's kind of a core feature in Nekoweb and so I wanted to use it. Nyscyra made a cool world map that shows the amount of visitors they have from each country. By the same devs for Nekoweb, they made Webtiles, a bunch of little 250 x 250 pixel sized websites you can make your own.

So, in short, I like Nekoweb.
